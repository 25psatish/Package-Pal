About Package Pal

Package Pal addresses critical employment disparities in the last-mile delivery industry through two fully functional collaborative robots (cobots): HelpingHandBot and Package Pal. With only 40.2% labor force participation among people with disabilities compared to 77.6% among those without disabilities, our innovative cobot solutions create new opportunities for inclusive employment.

As the global last-mile delivery market grows from $108.1 billion in 2020 to over $200 billion by 2027, our eco-friendly cobots integrate seamlessly with existing delivery vehicles, requiring minimal changes to current mail and delivery transportation systems.

HelpingHandBot, built entirely from LEGO Mindstorms parts, features ultrasonic sensors for mailbox distance detection, spike prime powered wheels for movement, and a high gear torque system for precise arm maneuvering. Package Pal, an upcycled remote control car with 3D printed conveyor belt and camera system, provides proof of delivery for packages up to 14 lbs.

With 19% of global purchases now transacted from homes, requiring 2 million delivery vehicles and drivers in the US alone, our solution enables disabled drivers to operate from their seats using remote controls and a Python-based mobile app, making last-mile delivery accessible to all.

Development Process

Discover the iterative journey behind Package Pal's creation, from initial research to functional prototypes

1

Initial Prototype Development

The first iteration of HelpingHandBot was derived from LEGO Technic models, featuring a basic design with the arm positioned behind the motor. This prototype was tested for mailbox distance accuracy and underwent multiple iterations to optimize arm length.

While the solution proved functional for mail delivery, it was incomplete as a similar mechanism was needed for larger packages, leading to the development of Package Pal.

2

Advanced Design & Engineering

Based on engineering design concepts learned in 2023 and with the intent to eventually productize, we created detailed exterior designs using FUSION 360 Software. This professional CAD modeling ensured precision and manufacturability.

To accelerate development, we repurposed the chassis of a remote-controlled car, bringing capability for basic movement across sidewalks and driveways. This innovative approach reduced development time while maintaining functionality.

3

Dual Prototype System

The final solution evolved into a dual prototype system: HelpingHandBot for mail delivery and Package Pal for larger packages. This comprehensive approach addresses the full spectrum of last-mile delivery needs.

Each stage incorporated iterative feedback from industry experts, peers, and end users, ensuring the solution meets real-world requirements and accessibility standards.
